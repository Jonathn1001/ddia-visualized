# Chapter 11 — Stream Processing: Debrief

## Same workload, three fates

You produced the same 12 messages into three brokers and watched the scoreboard
diverge. Nothing about the *messages* changed — the delivery guarantee fell out of
two decisions each broker makes:

- **What does the broker store?** A durable log (Kafka), a message held only until
  acked (RabbitMQ), or nothing at all (Redis pub/sub).
- **What does the consumer acknowledge?** A periodic offset commit (Kafka), a
  per-message ack (RabbitMQ), or nothing (Redis).

## Kafka — replayable log, at-least-once

The broker appends every message to a partition log and never deletes it. Consumers
commit an *offset* every few messages, so there is always a window of
processed-but-uncommitted work. Kill a consumer in that window and the partition is
reassigned and **replayed from the last commit** — the survivor reprocesses messages
the dead consumer already handled. That is the duplicate you made: `dup > 0, lost = 0`.
Exactly-once is not free here — it is idempotence or transactional offsets layered on
top of an at-least-once log.

## RabbitMQ — destructive queue, at-least-once

The broker deletes a message the instant it is acked — a destructive read, the
anti-log. If the ack never comes (consumer died, timeout fired), the message is
**requeued and redelivered** to another consumer, flagged `redelivered`. You resurrected
a message this way: `lost = 0`, and a duplicate appears only if the original consumer
processed it before its ack was lost. A message that fails too many times is
**dead-lettered** — parked, not lost. Per-message acks are finer-grained than Kafka's
per-offset commits, but the ceiling is the same: at-least-once.

## Redis pub/sub — fan-out, at-most-once

The broker stores **nothing**. It fans each message out to every live subscriber and
forgets it. A subscriber that is down misses the message forever; reviving it catches
only *future* publishes. That is the loss you produced: `dup = 0, lost > 0`. At-most-once
is not a bug — it is the direct consequence of a broker that keeps no log. (Redis
*Streams* add a log and change this story; pub/sub does not.)

## The one idea

> Delivery guarantees are a **storage** decision, not a delivery-mechanism trick.
> A log gives you replay (and duplicates); a destructive queue gives you redelivery;
> keeping nothing gives you loss. Pick the guarantee, and you have picked what the
> broker must store.

## Terms

*at-least-once* · *at-most-once* · *exactly-once* · *offset commit* · *consumer group* ·
*rebalance / partition reassignment* · *ack / redelivery* · *dead-letter* · *pub/sub
fan-out* — the stream-processing delivery vocabulary of DDIA Ch11.
